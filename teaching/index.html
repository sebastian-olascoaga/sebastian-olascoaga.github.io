Teaching

Graduate economics courses at MIT, on microeconomic theory, policy evaluation, and causal inference.

3 courses · MIT · 2019–2025

  • Instructor · MIT · Spring 2025

    Microeconomics

    A graduate course in microeconomic theory covering consumer and producer behavior, market equilibrium, and welfare analysis, with applications to urban policy. Topics include zoning, rent control, and licensing, alongside externalities, information asymmetries, and signaling drawn from housing and labor markets.

  • Instructor · MIT · Spring 2025

    Planning Economics

    Applies economic theory to policy evaluation and urban planning. Students work with empirical methods, causal inference, and econometric analysis to assess public programs, through problem sets built on real research articles and policy questions in housing, education, and public investment.

  • Head Teaching Assistant · MIT · 2019, 2022

    Quantitative Reasoning and Statistical Methods

    Statistical theory and causal inference for policy research. Covers randomized trials, regression, instrumental variables, difference-in-differences, regression discontinuity, and logistic regression.
