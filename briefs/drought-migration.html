In 74% of severe-drought metros, more Redfin users moved in than out.

Lily Katz · Sandoval-Olascoaga

Originally published · Redfin News ↗

In August 2022, $17 trillion worth of U.S. homes (roughly 25 million properties) sat in areas of intense drought. In 74% of the 34 metros where more than half of homes faced that level of drought, more Redfin users moved in than out during Q2 2022, led by Sacramento (+9,640), Las Vegas (+8,597), and San Antonio (+5,335).

The analysis uses migration patterns from about 2 million Redfin.com users searching across more than 100 metros in Q2 2022. Intense drought is defined as the severe, extreme, or exceptional categories from the U.S. Drought Monitor. The pattern held from the prior year: in Q2 2021, 16 of 23 severe-drought metros (70%) also had more inflows than outflows, even as the dollar value of drought-affected homes grew from $12 trillion to $17 trillion over those 12 months. For most of these movers, affordability took precedence over climate risk.

Drought may also not be scaring people off to the same extent as fires or flooding, which can physically decimate homes. Still, homeowners and buyers should be aware that drought danger could ultimately dent their home's value if a lack of water forces residents to leave en masse. Sandoval-Olascoaga, in the Redfin piece.
