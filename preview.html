I'm interested in whether rules and tools actually do what they promise — in housing markets meeting climate change, in city governments adopting new technology, in public-health policies under stress.

About

I do field experiments and evaluations on public questions. These days I lead the research and evaluation function at the City of Boston, where I run experiments on things like procurement AI and fare-free buses, and I teach the city-planning economics sequence at MIT DUSP.

Before all this: economic policy in Mexico City as Director of Economic Policy at the federal Treasury, the OECD in Paris, and fellowships at Redfin and OptumLabs working on housing markets, climate, and public health. I think in English, Spanish, and French, and try to write in all of them.
